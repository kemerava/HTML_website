Music genres
Genre Type More Info Link to a recourse
Symphony Classical An elaborate musical composition for full orchestra, typically in four movements, at least one of which is traditionally in sonata form. Beethoven was one of the most famous symphony composers. Greatest Symphonies
Rock'n'Roll Rock music A style of popular music that derives in part from blues and folk music and is marked by a heavily accented beat and a simple, repetitive phrase structure. Elvis Presley was famously called “The King of Rock 'n' Roll”. About Rock'n'Roll
Alternative Rock A Category of rock music that emerged from the independent music underground of the 1970s and became widely popular in the 1980s. "Alternative" refers to the genre's distinction from mainstream or commercial rock or pop music. The popular bands include Nirvana and Red Hot Chili Peppers Listen to Alt Rock here
Pop Popular Music A genre of popular music that originated in its modern form during the mid-1950s in the United States and the United Kingdom. It is the music that is most in line with the tastes and interests of the urban middle class. Michael Jackson is famously known as a "King of pop" History of Pop
Country a genre of popular music that takes its roots from genres such as blues and old-time music, and various types of American folk music and the cowboy Western music. Its popularized roots originate in the Southern United States of the early 1920s. Best of Country
Jazz Blue Note A musical form, often improvisational, that is often characterized by syncopated rhythms, polyphonic ensemble playing, varying degrees of improvisation, often deliberate deviations of pitch, and the use of original timbres. What's Jazz?
Blues Blues is an African-American music that traverses a wide range of emotions and musical styles. Central to the idea of blues performance is the concept that, by performing or listening to the blues, one is able to overcome sadness and lose the blues. If you feel blue
Techno Electronic dance music a genre of electronic dance music that is characterized by a repetitive four on the floor beat which is generally produced for use in a continuous DJ set. Artists may use electronic instruments such as drum machines, sequencers, and synthesizers, as well as digital audio workstations. Basics of EDM
